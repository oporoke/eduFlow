**What IEP actually is in CBC Kenya**

An Individualized Education Program (IEP) is a mandatory, personalized written document that a teacher must create for every learner ‚Äî especially those with special needs, learning difficulties, or unique learning styles. Under CBC, it spells out the child's learning needs, the services the school will provide, and how progress will be measured.

**Why it's a menace for teachers**

The real problem is scale and paperwork. Most Kenyan public primary school teachers have 60 to 80 learners in their classrooms. CBC requires continuous, individualized assessment for each of them. Designing, administering, and marking regular formative assessments was noted as particularly challenging with the existing student-teacher ratios. Teachers are essentially expected to write and maintain a personalized education plan for every single student ‚Äî by hand, on paper, on top of lesson planning, teaching, and marking. It's an enormous, time-consuming burden that many educators feel underprepared for.

**My Proposed Solution: AI-Powered IEP Module for EduFlow**

Here's how I'd make it innovative and practical:

**1. Auto-Generated IEP Drafts**
Based on a student's quiz scores, lesson progress, and pace feedback from presenter mode, the system automatically generates a draft IEP for each student. The teacher reviews and approves rather than writing from scratch. This alone cuts 80% of the paperwork.

**2. Student Learning Profile**
Every student gets a dynamic profile that tracks: lessons completed, quiz performance over time, pace votes (Too Fast / Lost), and comment activity. This data feeds the IEP automatically.

**3. IEP Builder**
A guided form where the teacher fills in: student strengths, areas of concern, learning goals, recommended interventions, and review dates. Pre-filled suggestions come from the student's activity data.

**4. Parent Portal**
Parents can view their child's IEP and progress report without needing to come to school. They can acknowledge receipt digitally ‚Äî solving the parental engagement problem CBC also highlights.

**5. IEP Review Reminders**
The system reminds teachers when an IEP is due for review, and flags students who are falling behind (low quiz scores + low lesson completion) so no student slips through the cracks.

**6. Export to PDF**
Teachers can export any IEP as a clean, formatted PDF for official submission or printing.
